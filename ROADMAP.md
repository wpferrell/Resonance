# Resonance — Roadmap

*Last updated: April 2026*

This document is the single source of truth for everything Resonance is building toward. It covers the architecture, the build queue, the training pipeline, and all planned updates to public-facing surfaces.

---

## What Resonance Is Building Toward

Resonance sits invisibly in any conversation and knows how a person actually feels — not what they say, but what is underneath. It gets more accurate per person over time. It makes any LLM conversation feel like something that genuinely knows you are there.

Every decision in this roadmap traces back to that vision.

## Completed — April 2026

### Dataset Rules — Revised R1–R7

All seven dataset acquisition rules have been reviewed and rewritten to better reflect Resonance's vision and future commercial trajectory.

| Rule | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| R1 | Commercial license only (MIT, CC-BY, Apache 2.0, CC0, etc.). CC-BY-SA permitted but flagged for legal review before any enterprise licensing deal. |
| R2 | Peer-reviewed or major institution source. Applies to external public datasets only — Resonance's own collected user feedback data is exempt. |
| R3 | The text must be real human expression — written or spoken by actual people, not generated by AI. AI-assisted labelling on real human text is permitted on a case-by-case basis. Fully AI-generated text is a hard fail. |
| R4 | Must contain text in which real humans are expressing, describing, or processing emotional or psychological states. Labels do not need to match our taxonomy exactly — adjacent signals (stress, distress, wellbeing, mood, mental health) qualify if the text itself carries emotional content. Excludes domains with no emotional signal. |
| R5 | No domain too narrow. Psychological, emotional, and mental health domains are never considered too narrow for Resonance — they are the core domain. |
| R6 | No gated access — if obtaining the data requires registration, approval, or a data use agreement, it is excluded regardless of what the license says. |
| R7 | No severe structural class imbalance caused by the collection method itself. Natural real-world class imbalance is acceptable and handled via class weighting at training time. |

**ISEAR dropped** — R1 fail. University of Geneva explicitly restricts to non-commercial research use only.

**Stack: v7, 30 datasets, ~1,490,000 rows. Legally clean.**

**Permanently excluded:** LIWC, EmpatheticDialogues, DailyDialog, RECCON

### Psychology Frameworks — Revised from 8 to 6

The frameworks have been revised to reflect only what Resonance actually detects — signals that produce real output. Two items moved to the response layer as design principles.

**6 Detection Frameworks:**

| # | Framework | What It Detects | Status |
|---|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| 1 | SDT | Autonomy, competence, relatedness signals in text | Build |
| 2 | DBT Primary/Secondary | Primary emotion + independently detected secondary emotion | Build |
| 3 | Reappraisal/Suppression | Scored floats — how the person is relating to their emotion | Build |
| 4 | Window of Tolerance | Regulated / hyperaroused / hypoaroused — session trajectory | Build |
| 5 | PERMA | Five wellbeing pillars — positive emotion, engagement, relationships, meaning, accomplishment | Build |
| 6 | Wise Mind | Scored float — emotional mind / wise mind / reasonable mind balance | Build |

**Response Layer Principles (not detection signals):**
- Validate before problem-solve — always acknowledge before solving
- Non-judgment — no emotion is good or bad inside Resonance

**Longitudinal inference (not a framework):**
- Attachment style — builds silently from SDT, suppression, and WoT signals across 10+ sessions. Not directly detected. Never claimed.

**Design principle (runs across all frameworks):**
- Trajectory — Resonance always tracks direction of travel, not just current state. Are things getting better or worse?

### Teacher-Student Model Architecture — Locked

**Three teachers** train independently on the full dataset stack:
- Teacher 1: DeBERTa-v3-large + CNN head (specialist in shame, guilt, local patterns)
- Teacher 2: XLNet-large (specialist in anger, contextual dependencies)
- Teacher 3: ELECTRA-large (specialist in fear, efficient signal extraction)

Each teacher produces soft probability distributions across all 10 active output types. An ensemble script combines them with confidence weighting and temperature scaling into one final soft label per training example.

**Student: DeBERTa-v3-base**
- 86M backbone parameters, 12 layers, 768 hidden dimensions
- 10 active heads trained simultaneously + 3 deferred heads
- Trains on soft labels from teacher ensemble + hard labels + focal loss + contrastive loss

**10 Active Student Output Heads:**

| Category | Heads |
|----------|-------|
| Core | Emotion (7 classes), VAD (continuous V/A/D), Confidence calibration, CNN local patterns |
| Frameworks | Secondary emotion (21 TONE classes), PERMA ×5 (continuous), Reappraisal/suppression, Wise Mind |
| Ethics | Crisis detection, Alexithymia detection |

**3 Deferred Heads (designed but not trained in this phase):**
- Window of Tolerance — threshold recalibration handles this more accurately than per-message classification
- Sustained distress — session counter in storage handles this better than a per-message head
- Wise Mind session-level — session-level phenomenon, not reliably detectable per message

**2 Encoder training objectives:**
- Contrastive loss on shame vs neighbouring emotions (shame separation)
- Domain adversarial training across all source datasets (prevents domain language from dominating)

**Training objectives:**
- Multi-task loss weighted across all 10 active heads
- Focal loss on hard examples (shame, guilt)
- Contrastive loss on shame vs neighbouring emotions
- Domain adversarial training (prevents domain-specific language from dominating)

**ONNX and personal adaptation stack (GatedBias, LoRA, ATLAS, federated delta):**
Parked until the above is fully working and validated. Will be picked up in a later phase.

## Build Queue — 5 Phases

### Phase 1 — Design (before any code)

1. Lock 10-head active student architecture — exact specification of what each head outputs, what each teacher generates, what each loss function optimises. Confirm 3 deferred heads and rationale. Confirm 2 encoder objectives.
2. Lock validation criteria for each subsequent phase — what "working" looks like before moving on

### Phase 2 — Data Pipeline

3. Add 2 pending datasets to Registry first, then script: Reddit MH MDPI 2024 (800 rows CC-BY), SWMH (AIMH/SWMH, 54,412 rows, R5 flag)
4. Check scout_results.md for any new scout finds — add any passes to Registry first
5. Build unified `scripts/build_training_data.py` — three steps in one script:
   - Deduplication across all datasets
   - Soft labels derived from VAD scores and NRC affect frequencies
   - Back-translation augmentation for shame and guilt rows only (real text, not synthetic — meaning preserved through linguistic transformation)

### Phase 3 — Extractor Framework Signals

Build all 6 framework signals into `resonance/extractor.py`. Each must be working and producing verified output before training begins.

6. PERMA — connect existing proprietary lexicon to extractor, 5 new scored fields on EmotionResult
7. SDT — 3 new detection signals: `autonomy_signal`, `competence_signal`, `relatedness_signal`
8. WoT trajectory — session-level tracking, direction of travel across a session
9. Secondary emotion independence — detected in parallel with primary, not inferred from it
10. Reappraisal/suppression scored floats — convert from booleans, add suppression to distress trajectory
11. Wise Mind scored float — convert from boolean
12. Ethics safeguards revision — update sustained distress trigger to factor in suppression float and WoT trajectory
13. Trajectory layer — direction of travel signal across all 6 frameworks per session
14. MLE emoji lexicon — replace crude `EMOJI_MAP` in extractor with the Multidimensional Lexicon of Emojis (Godard & Holtzman 2022, Frontiers in Psychology, CC-BY). 359 emojis rated on 8 NRC emotion dimensions. Extends NRC Emotion Lexicon into emoji territory.

**Validation checkpoint** — all 6 frameworks tested on real text, output verified before moving to Phase 4

### Phase 4 — Train

15. DAPT — domain-adaptive pretraining on full dataset before teacher training
16. Teacher model training — 3 teachers, ensemble soft label generation
17. **Validation checkpoint** — soft label quality verified before student training
18. Multi-task loss weighting — balanced across all 10 active heads
19. Student distillation training — DeBERTa-v3-base, 10 active heads, all training objectives
20. Confidence profile update — extended to capture framework signal frequency

**Validation checkpoint** — all 10 active heads tested against real text, shame F1 checked specifically, output verified before Phase 5

### Phase 5 — Integrate

21. Extractor refactor — model output drives EmotionResult directly, VAD lookup table retired, no fallback
22. Feedback system — full EmotionResult + trajectory context captured, auto-consent verified
23. Storage update — all new EmotionResult fields captured in Qdrant and SurrealDB
24. Pi server update — full EmotionResult capture, trajectory endpoint, export pipeline
25. Attachment inference layer — builds silently from SDT, suppression, and WoT trajectory across 10+ sessions
